Competitions

INTRODUCTION

ICB will hold two partner-based virtual competitions in 2024 at the international level. The first round is a qualifier round while the second will be the final round featuring a $500 prize pool. Each question given to competitors will be on advanced chemistry and related subtopics. The competition is free to enter with the qualifying round for the International competition taking place on 2/3/2024 and the final round taking place on 2/10/2024.

Registration Process

The guide below will detail the important steps when registering for competitions.

1
Go to the Upcoming Competitions section on the Home page.
2
Click on the competition you're interested in.
3
Fill out and submit the registration form.
4
Check your inbox for an email containing details two weeks prior to the competition.

    Rules and Regulations

    Make sure to follow all of the listed rules to ensure a smooth competition process.

    1. ICB will hold two partner-based virtual competitions in 2024 at the international level.
    2. The competition will consist of two rounds: a qualifier round and a final round.
      • Qualified participants will receive follow-up emails about the data and time of the final round.
    3. The final round will feature a prize pool of $500.
    4. All questions given to competitors will be on advanced chemistry and related subtopics.
    5. The competition will cover the following topics, with the percentage indicating the approximate weight given to each topic:
      • Inorganic Chemistry: 30%
      • Biochemistry: 20%
      • Physical Chemistry: 20%
      • Organic Chemistry: 15%
      • Analytical Chemistry: 7.5%
      • Miscellaneous (Astrochemistry, Materials Science, etc.): 7.5%
    6. The participants should be aware of the rules and regulations of the competition and abide by them.
    7. The competition can be held in different languages, but the questions will be in English.
      • Translated question books may be made available if the request is made 30 days prior to the competition.
    8. The participants should be aware of the schedule and the deadline of the competition and abide by them.
    9. The participants are not allowed to collaborate or share any information during the competition.
    10. The results of the competition will be based on the scores obtained by the participants in the two rounds.
      • The top 50 teams from the qualifying round will qualify to the final round with opportunities to win cash prizes.
